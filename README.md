# Superstore Sales Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Superstore dataset to uncover sales trends, profitability patterns, and business insights. The analysis focuses on understanding the factors that drive profit and loss across product categories and identifying opportunities for business improvement.

A special case study was conducted on the **Tables** and **Bookcases** sub-categories, which consistently generated losses despite contributing significant sales.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze overall sales and profit performance.
- Identify profitable and loss-making categories.
- Investigate the impact of discounts on profitability.
- Understand relationships between sales, quantity, discounts, and profit.
- Perform a focused business analysis on Tables and Bookcases.
- Generate actionable business recommendations using data.

## 📌 Special Case Study: Tables & Bookcases Analysis

A detailed investigation was conducted on the Tables and Bookcases sub-categories to understand why they frequently generated losses.

### Analysis Performed

- Sales vs Profit Analysis
- Discount vs Profit Analysis
- Transaction-Level Loss Investigation
- Correlation Analysis
- High-Loss Order Identification

### Key Findings

- Tables and Bookcases experienced recurring losses despite maintaining healthy sales volumes.
- Higher discounts significantly reduced profitability.
- Several high-revenue transactions still resulted in losses because of aggressive discounting.
- Profitability was negatively affected even when sales increased.

### Business Recommendation

- Reduce excessive discounts for Tables and Bookcases.
- Reassess pricing strategies for low-margin products.
- Closely monitor transactions with high discount percentages.
- Focus on profit-driven sales strategies rather than sales volume alone.

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

- Strong sales do not always result in high profits.
- Discounts have a significant impact on profitability.
- Tables and Bookcases are among the least profitable sub-categories.
- Certain products generate losses despite high sales values.
- Profit optimization requires better discount management.

---

## 📈 Business Insights

Based on the analysis, the company can improve profitability by:

- Optimizing discount policies.
- Reviewing pricing strategies for low-margin products.
- Prioritizing profitable product categories.
- Monitoring loss-making transactions.
- Implementing profit-focused sales strategies.
